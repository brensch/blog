Every site needs a first post, so here's mine.

I spend most of my time building things that are a little too elaborate for
their own good — game prototypes, a homelab held together with Docker and
stubbornness, and small tools that solve problems only I have.

The plan for this place is simple: when I figure something out that took me
longer than it should have, I'll write it down here. No schedule, no
promises. We'll see if I ever update it.

> The best time to start a blog was ten years ago. The second best time is
> right before you get distracted by something shinier.

More soon. Or not. Either way, thanks for stopping by.
